### Maintenance Tips üîß
1. **After playing**: Swab out moisture with a soft cloth on a thin rod ‚Äî never leave wet
2. **Oil regularly**: Use coconut oil, almond oil, or mustard oil every 2-4 weeks
   - Apply with a soft cloth through the bore, up to the blow hole only
   - Oil the finger holes and blow hole edges with a Q-tip
   - **Never oil a wet/freshly played bansuri** ‚Äî let it dry first
3. **Storage**: Keep in carry bag, away from direct sunlight and extreme heat/cold
4. **Avoid AC vents**: Dry air can crack bamboo
5. **Check threading**: The string binding can loosen over time ‚Äî get it re-wound if needed
6. **Break-in period**: Play for short sessions initially (15-20 min), gradually increase
7. **Mumbai humidity**: Actually good for bamboo! But avoid leaving near windows during monsoon

### Stability vs Neutral: The Debate

**For flat feet, stability shoes are generally recommended**, but it depends on your type:

- **Flexible flat feet** (arch visible when not weight-bearing) ‚Üí **Stability shoes recommended**, but stable neutrals can work for mild overpronation
- **Rigid flat feet** (arch never visible) ‚Üí **Stability shoes strongly recommended**

**Key stability features to look for:**
- Medial post (prevents inward rolling)
- Dual-density midsole
- Wide, stiff platform
- Stiff heel counter
- Sole flares

**Quick test**: Stand on tiptoes ‚Äî if arches appear, you have flexible flat feet.
